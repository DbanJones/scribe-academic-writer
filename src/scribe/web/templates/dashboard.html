What are you working on?

Three modes, picked by where you are in the writing process. Each uses Claude against the same unified academic writing rules (Allwood, Williams, Pinker, Sword, Schimel, Zinsser, et al.).

Write

Start a new paper

You have an outline and sources. Scribe analyses the thesis, plans chunks, writes sections in parallel, and stitches them.

Use when: you have a structured outline (headings and bullets) and reference material, but no prose yet.

New project →
Revise

Sharpen an existing draft

Audit a finished document against the writing rules, then revise it section by section without changing length. Citations, numbers, claims, and figures preserved exactly.

Use when: your draft is the right length but the prose could be tighter, more concrete, or more concise.

Revise a document →
Expand

Deepen an existing draft

Take a substantive draft and extend it into a longer, deeper version. Scribe unpacks compressed passages, surfaces evidence, and spells out mechanisms — never invents claims.

Use when: you have a summary or short draft and need it two or three times longer for a chapter or journal submission.

Expand a document →
Restyle

Change citation style

Convert any finished paper between Harvard, IEEE, APA, Nature, Vancouver, Chicago, or a journal-specific preset you define.

Use when: the document is done but the target journal wants a different citation format.

Open restyler →
